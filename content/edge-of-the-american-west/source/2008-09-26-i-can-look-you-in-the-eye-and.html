second, if true, should it affect anyone’s vote? surely not in gore’s case; he was a better candidate in every respect, even if he had spent the whole debate addressing bush as “governor stupid fuck lame-brain”. so i worry that once again we are seeing the media focus on entirely irrelevant matters of style and personality and earth-tones when they are failing to do their job on issues and ideas.

Jay C

kb: by not watching the debate on TV, you may have missed the all-important visual effect of Sen. McCain’s non-eye-contact posturing at the podium. Sen. Obama, on viewer’s right, usually kept his eyes straight ahead (at the camera), or turned half-right, to Jim Lehrer (in the center), or full-right (at. Sen. McCain). McCain, though, on viewer’s left, also kept his eyes mostly turned to the right while speaking, which gave the effect of him talking into space, or away from the moderator: which was magnified by the typical split-screen shots of the candidates. Obama was mostly looking respectfully at the “audience”, or his opponent: McCain looked like he was talking to the air. Not the visuals you really want to display: and I think that the visuals were what was really going to matter in this debate. Hence the “advantage: Obama” conclusion so many have reached.

To circle back to the Kid’s original point, my feeling last night was that McCain had done better. Obama sometimes has trouble completing points. He’ll set up a critique, and you can see where it’s going, but then he doesn’t drive it home.

McCain is the fragile patriarch — warm and generous when his authority is conceded, bitterly angry when not. This is a guy famous for cursing out fellow Republican senators. Obama is a conciliator. And this is, for all the mockery of community organizing, part of the community organizer’s skill: you work out common ground, get people talking, and narrow differences where you can, because you *can’t* pull authority.
