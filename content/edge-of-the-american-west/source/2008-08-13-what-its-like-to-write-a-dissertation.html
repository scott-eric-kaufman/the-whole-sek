What it’s like to write a dissertation

August 13, 2008 in history and current events

True dat. Probably no consolation, but I’ll say it anyway (as a parallel metaphor): the psychology of writing a dissertation is like moving a mountain of manure, one shovel at a time. You know that if you can get it moved from one part of the land to another, it’ll be transformed (by shifting context) from a large pile of shit that may eventually help new things grow. And it’s good work to do. But it’s a damned big pile, and it stinks, and all you’ve got is a shovel. If you thought about how many shovels-ful you were going to have to move, you might give up and go play video-games.

But you resolve to move just that day’s part of the pile, and not think too hard about the total number of days remaining. And one day you will look up and find the whole pile is moved. And it is remarkable how swiftly that completion will yield unforeseen positive results, for yourself and others.
