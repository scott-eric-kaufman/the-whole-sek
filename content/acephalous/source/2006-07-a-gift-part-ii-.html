A Gift, Part II: This Time, It's Illustrated

I'm not going to put it on the site, but I want anyone in the mood for something brilliant to throw me a line and the benefit of the doubt.  I've finished the first twelve issues of Warren Ellis's Planetary, and if you can stomach one egregiously lame "revelation," I'm sure you'll agree it's the one of the best damned thing you've read in some time.  You'll have to think to the left, the right, before and behind "the box" to do so, but I guarantee the effort'll be worth the reward. 

So if you're an honorable person who fancies novel stimuli, send me an email (at any of the addresses I haunt) and I'll send you a substantial something or other.

IRONIC CODA: If you've sent me an email in the past three weeks and I have yet to respond, I apologize.  Earlier this evening, I discovered approximately seventy emails in Gmail's spam folder which don't belong there.  I'm in the process of responding, but have serious doubts about my stamina, so if you don't hear from me tonight, please don't add it to your tally of my inequities.  I'm hard at type rehabilitating my good name, and the wonky vibes you'd be broadcasting could bring this whole show crashing down.

Although the violence which with his case concluded is anomalous, this transcript succinctly captures not only the cultural milieu of Summer 2003, but also its continued (and possibly salutary) resonance. Focus on the experience of breathing, the feel of cool air over your lips, into your lungs, then out again. Now listen to the smooth, soothing sound of my words as they wash over your worries and fears. You are in a safe space, now. Nothing from the grind can disturb the peace you now inhabit now. You lose yourself in the warm embrace, of my calming words, and you breathe deeply, and you breathe slowly, and you feel the cool air cross your lips . . . . . . and you look around you, at your surroundings. You are in a familiar place, but you experience it from an angle that makes it seem unfamiliar now. Tell me, [#3987], what do you see? I hear Kenny Mayne shouting. He sounds scared. Someone else is shouting too. "The devils!" they scream, and together they scream, "The devils!"1 Where am I? You're in a safe place, remember.
