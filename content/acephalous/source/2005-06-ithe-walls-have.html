The Walls Have Ears, a Diverimento for Four Trombones and Hidden Mike

After digging up the Stanislaw Lem quotation to throw at Jonathan today, I decided that--what with it being the start of that period responsible academics call  "vacation" but mean "the beginning of the self-imposed toil"--I'd re-read the whole damn book.  That's right.  Appropos of abso-fucking-lutely nothing, I spent the day reading Memoirs Found in a Bathtub.  It was as entertaining and ultimately predictable as I remember, but I found the introduction far more compelling than I had in years past.  To wit:

Those finds concern religious beliefs prevalent during the Eight Dynasty of Ammer-Ka [20th Cenutry America]; they speak of various Perils--Black, Red, Yellow--evidently cabalistic incantations connected in some way with the mysterious deity Rayss, to whom burnt offerings were apparently made. (6)

Worship of the deity Kap-Eh-Taahl...became one of the dominant cults of the time.  This deity was revered throughout Ammer-Ka and the faith quickly spread to Australindia and parts of the European Peninsula.  Any connection, however, between the cult of Kap-Eh-Taahl and the graven images of the elephant and the ass found here and there throughout Ammer-Ka does seem doubtful.  It was forbidden to utter the name itself, "Kap-Eh-Taahl" (analogous to the Hebrew interdiction); in Ammer-Ka the deity was generally called "Al-mighty Da-Laahr."  (7)

I fault that second passage because of that parenthetical insertion of information an anthropologist in the far, far future--especially one who "mistakes" capitalism for a religion--probably wouldn't have known.  But it's amusing nonetheless.  I'm beginning to think that with how uninteresting this post is turning out, I may have been better of not blogging at all today.  C'est la vie.  You're the one still reading...
