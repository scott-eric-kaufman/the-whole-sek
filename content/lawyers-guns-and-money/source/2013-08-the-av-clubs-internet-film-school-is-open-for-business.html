      The AV Club’s Internet Film School is open for business!*

      [ 36 ] August 29, 2013 | SEK

      It’s the first day of class, and I’m starting really basic. As in really basic. That’s where the best courses begin.

      Attendance and participation are both mandatory. I want to see comments from familiar faces in these new strange places. Because if it weren’t for you, I wouldn’t have this new gig. Engaging with you lot made me want to investigate this mode of writing, so you owe me.

      You owe me.

      Now go make me look good in front of my new bosses. What? Really?

      Fine—I also want to say thank you for all the encouragement over the years. I wouldn’t have this opportunity if it weren’t for you.

      *Facebook title of this same announcement: “HOLY SHIT-BALLS-IN-A-CAN I WRITE FOR THE ONION.”

        • SEK says:
          August 29, 2013 at 7:27 am

          Somewhere, does Scorcese say “I closed Godfather II to show that Michael Corleone is a piece of shit, and used framing X, Y, and Z to do so.”

          That would be very interesting, given that Coppola directed Godfather II. But in all seriousness, basically every director/cinematographer’s commentary reel addresses the issue of framing either directly or, more commonly, indirectly. Every time a director talks about lighting, or about set design, or about camera angle, he or she’s also talking about framing, because the real issue with lighting isn’t just that a scene is “properly lit,” but that it’s lit in a manner that captures the mood the director intends for what’s on frame. (Put differently: there’s no such thing as “properly lit,” only “usefully,” and the measure of usefulness is whether it does the work the director requires of a given shot.)
