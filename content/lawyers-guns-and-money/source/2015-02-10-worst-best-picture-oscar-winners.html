            I am actually going to completely disagree with this.

            It’s funny how much the stock of “Dances With Wolves” has fallen in recent years. But at the time (1990), this movie was seen (judging from critics reviews) as one of the greatest films of all time.

            I’m with you. Coincidentally I actually watched it this weekend for the first time since it came out, showing it to my kids. It was the extended version – almost 4 hours – and the extra time didn’t add much to the story and may have contributed to it’s reduction in popularity over time, as that is what you get when you rent it these days. I get that it’s now spat upon by a lot of liberals as another example of the White Savior storyline. But that isn’t true – Dunbar wasn’t a savior. The usual White Savior movies have the white person showing the natives how they can live their lives much better and/or win the battle against the whites. None of that happened in the movie – it was Dunbar who changed how he lived his life, not the other way around. Yes, at one point in the movie he provides rifles to the tribe to fend off an attack from another tribe. At another point the tribe rescues him. Not at all the normal “White Savior” storyline.

            Honestly, I think that these discussions tend to descend into contra-ism where a lot of posters compete to see who can be first to shit on an item of popular culture to prove how really elite they are. “What, you think ‘Like a Rolling Stone” was good Dylan? Crap – you don’t know good Dylan until you have heard (insert reference to extremely obscure late-era Dylan album).”

            In the case of Dances with Wolves, I think the reduction in popularity is a mix of two factors. First, it came out at a time when the right wing media infrastructure was just getting warmed up – only 3 years after the Fairness Doctrine was killed off allowing right wingers to take over AM radio and, a few years later, much of cable TV. The reich wing HATED Dances With Wolves with every fiber of their beings, for all of the obvious reasons. And they’ve done every thing they could for the past 25 years to knock it down. What the reich wing does well is inject little memes into the mainstream that average people buy. Like “California has budget problems because it’s so liberal” or “Al Gore talks environment but wastes energy in his huge mansion”. They also succeeded with Dances with Wolves, in which the average person who never has seen the movie has thus “heard” that it unfairly represents the white cavalry.

            Then add in the liberals who shit on the movie to prove their bona fides and, well, its decline in popularity is inevitable.

            In fact, for a 1990 movie the cinematography was terrific, and the attempt to represent prairie life accurately was state-of-the-art. The representation of Sioux life and culture was praised by their descendants. The story itself was extremely compelling to contemporaneous audiences. Unlike, say, Avatar, the natives were not shown as all good nor the conquering whites as all bad. Some criticized the movie for the portrayal of Pawnees as overly violent and aggressive – which has been a common portrayal in recent white stories of the period from Little Big Man to Centennial. Some criticized the movie for John Dunbar overly romanticizing the lifestyle of the Sioux – that was the character’s viewpoint, but the movie was from his point of view as narrator.

            I wouldn’t argue that it was the greatest picture that year or, indeed, argue it’s greatness at all. These are all extremely subjective ratings and arguing like there is some sort of objective measure of the greatness of an artwork is something I just can’t do. But I do think the criticisms from the left are way too much and probably say more about the critics than they do about the movie.
