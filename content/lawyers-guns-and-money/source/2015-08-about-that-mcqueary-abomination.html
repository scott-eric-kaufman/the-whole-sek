                    Right, so Chicago and Denver are very similar in this regard. There are a number of good elementary schools (Cory, Asbury, etc. in Denver; South Loop, Ray, Holden in Chicago), but they typically feed into crappy junior highs. But both districts have some awesome magnets. The only problem is that the school districts don’t want those magnets to be made up almost exclusively by the kids from the rich neighborhoods (with the good elementary schools) — the whole point of a magnet is to get a diverse student body from around the district. So the number of slots from the rich neighborhoods are extremely limited, and it’s next to impossible to get in if you’re from there. So what we’ve seen is a lot of parents take the whole “magnet or move to the burbs” approach, and like 75% of them move to the burbs. Ironically, if you do that for your oldest kid, you’re very likely taking a step down for your younger kids in elementary school.
