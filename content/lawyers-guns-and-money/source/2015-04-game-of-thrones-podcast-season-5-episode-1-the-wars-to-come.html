                  The thing you gotta understand is that there are two reasons that the first three books were pounded out with two-years gaps between them and the last two had six-year gaps, and the second one, I feel, is directly related to why Dance with Dragons seems so scattershot.

                  The first is that Martin suddenly leapt from “obscure mid-list SF/F author, has a couple Hugo’s but cannot really be called a contender” to “holy shit, he’s a big deal.” Some authors, this wouldn’t affect output. Other authors… Martin is very blunt about the fact that he can’t write while on the road, in hotel rooms, on a plane, etc. He has to be at home in his den, mind focused. That’s hard when you have an increasingly aggressive appearance schedule.

                  But that’s me going way off on a tangent.

                  The second thing is that Martin wrote himself into a brick wall, backed up, wrote himself into ANOTHER brick wall, and then went “fuck it” and just smashed on through. Originally there was supposed to be a huge time-skip (like five, six years) between SoS and Dance with Dragons. Then he realized he’d written about four hundred pages of flashbacks covering that time period and was like “goddammit.” Went back, tried again. Ended up in an enormous tangle with what was going on in Essos. Went way over page count. Split the book in two, hiving off the stuff in the southern half of Westeros that “worked” from the stuff in the North and Essos that wasn’t.

                  Then he slammed into another tangle with how the fuck he wanted Meereen to work. Took him ages to work through that as well.

                  So what you’re seeing in Dance with Dragons is the wreckage of a decade-plus of trying to salvage a ton of plotting and plotlines that Martin realized as early as 2002 just were NOT working as originally planned. Because he is a very good writer it mostly still works, but if you know the history behind it you can see it’s all a bit spackle and bailing wire.

                  That’s my take on it, at any rate.
