          • Peterr says:
            June 17, 2015 at 11:49 am

            It’s not copyright infringement, as long as they make arrangements through ASCAP (or whoever services the copyrights) and pay the appropriate fees, but ASCAP does not (to my knowledge) screen for anything else. Their whole purpose is to see that artists get paid for the use of their music. Artists sign up with ASCAP for that purpose, and it generally serves them very well.

            But getting paid for music being used is very different from having your music associated with a political cause (of any stripe). That’s probably why conservatives seem to get into more problems with this than liberals. The key point for conservatives is making sure the legal I’s have been dotted and T’s crossed, and they simply can’t conceive that an artist would have a problem with anyone using a given song as long as they get paid for it. “Hey, you got your Benjamins — why are you whining?”

            For non-conservatives, on the other hand, there is a recognition that there is more than money that drives what people do (especially artists and creative-types), and thus they are a bit more likely to at least consider the question “Does artist X actually seem to be in synch with what we’re doing, or are they likely to make a big public stink about having their work twisted in ways they don’t like?”
