          The Greek yogurt version of whey isn’t high protein like the cheese version of whey is. Greek yogurt is high protein because the straining process leaves practically all of the protein in the Greek yogurt. And even if it were worthwhile to process the waste further for any leftover protein, you still have to deal with the high acidity of the residue.

          In fact, I’m thinking that this regulation might be tangentially related to Greek yogurt. One method of disposing of Greek yogurt waste is to feed it to livestock. Problem is that livestock can consume only so much low-protein acidic feed at a time, and if you store the whey for long, who knows what would grow in there. I can easily see the feds moving in to regulate, and the Greek yogurt people saying wait a minute, what about spent grain from beer making? (Incidentally, spent grain is almost certainly safer as is than Greek yogurt whey; much less moisture for the wee beasties to work with, and more or less presanitized from the wort-making process.)
