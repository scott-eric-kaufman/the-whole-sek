      Recommend me some music, please.

      [ 50 ] December 23, 2010 | SEK

      The West thread below and its kin at Pandagon have yielded some excellent recommendations for music I should listen to on the long drive from rural Mississippi to the northern outskirts of Houston, but just in case those solid souls missed something essential, I thought I’d ask:

      What should I have listened to last year?

      Feel free to base your recommendations on the assumptions that 1) my taste is wildly eclectic and 2) the only album I bought was originally an LP pressed the year before I was born … because that’s close enough to the truth for government work.

      31. Henry Holland says:
        December 24, 2010 at 3:34 pm

        All that angst-y rock music! You’ll need to decompress, so how’s about some classical music?

        Since classical recordings made in 1952 still sell, I consider “this year” to be an arbitrary metric.

        Bach: Toccatas and Fugues (Angela Hewitt)
        Lovely Bach piano playing, though written for harpsichord

        Rachmaninov: Symphonies (LSO, Previn)
        Beautiful, lush, melodic scores that more than a few film composers have pilfered from

        Chopin: Anything by Chopin (Rubinstein)
        Essential piano music

        Bax: Tone Poems Vol. 1 & 2 (BBCPO, Handley)
        Gorgeous English pastoralism from the underrated Bax

        Beethoven: Symphonies (Berlin Phil, Karajan 1962)
        Um, it’s Beethoven, duh

        Schreker: Orchestral Works Vol. 1 & 2 (BBCPO, Sinaisky)
        Gorgeous Impressionist/Strauss influenced works by the great Austrian opera composer

        Dvorak: Symphonic Poems (Czech PO, Makerras)
        Haunting, tuneful works, perfect for night driving

        Das ist genug…..
