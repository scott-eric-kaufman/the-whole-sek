      SEK’s Big Adventure, Part Whatever-It-Is-At-This-Point-I-Give-Up

      [ 39 ] June 4, 2016 | SEK

      Today started weird, then got weirder:

      SEK: Hello, Mr. Rooster.

      ROOSTER ON SEK’S PORCH: (walks like roosters own the place)

      SEK: Why are you on my porch?

      ROOSTER ON SEK’S PORCH: (walks like roosters own the place)

      SEK: Don’t you have someplace you belong?

      ROOSTER ON SEK’S PORCH: (walks like roosters own the place)

      SEK: You know there’s both a TINY OUTSIDE CAT and a SLIGHTLY LARGER OUTSIDE CAT who frequent this place?

      ROOSTER ON SEK’S PORCH: (walks like roosters own the place)

      SEK: You don’t understand a fucking word I’m saying, do you?

      ROOSTER ON SEK’S PORCH: (walks like roosters own the place)

      SEK: Get off my porch, Mr. Rooster.

      ROOSTER ON SEK’S PORCH: (walks like roosters own the place)

      SEK: Seriously, get off my —

      ROOSTER ON SEK’S PORCH: (ENTERS EXTREME DEATH ROOSTER ATTACK MODE)

      SEK: OK I WILL GO INSIDE YOU CONTINUE TO ROOSTER OUT HERE AS LONG AS YOU’D LIKE

      ROOSTER ON SEK’S PORCH: (walks like roosters own the place)

              Some 20 years ago we moved to the country, with 2 or 3 cats. Before long I rehabbed the chicken coop, and we bought some hens from a neighbor over the mountain. We got a rooster from the old boys next door – he was huge.

              One of our cats was really big too, nearly 20 pounds, and full of himself. We had a little footbridge over the creek towards the chicken coop. One day I saw Ralph (the really big, white with orange spots tomcat) getting down on one end of the bridge, bracing his hind feet and getting ready to pounce – the huge red rooster at the other end of the 12 foot bridge.

              When Ralph pounced, the rooster spread his wings and rose into the air, easily twice as big as he was when Ralph launched himself in attack.

              When he landed in the middle of the bridge, Ralph was already in full reverse. He swapped ends and dug in to speed away from the rooster, who had his talons spread out in front.

              So I know about your rooster.

              Years ago my parents were driving through Delaware on a vacation drive, and went into a park-museum, once a duPont estate. There was a pond with swans, and the folks stopped to take a picture and admire the scene. Then one of the swans headed out towards them…

              Mom eventually said “That bird is coming for you!” just as Dad hit the button to raise his window, then the swan hit the glass, leaving swan spit spattered on the window. They were so glad they didn’t have the top down on that roadster!!

              Birds ARE dinosaurs !!
