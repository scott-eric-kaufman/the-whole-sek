              • Murc says:
                April 27, 2015 at 1:00 pm

                How are we defining limits, though?

                Like, I’m pretty sure Littlefinger would love to have godlike powers over everything. In that sense, his ambition doesn’t have any limits.

                But there’s little practical way to achieve that goal, and even if there were it would probably involve insane quests to the ends of the world (places like Stygai and K’Dath) that even he would recognize as not being worth the risk.

                I think Littlefinger would love to be King Petyr I Baelish. He might even be good at it. But I don’t think he actually has the capability to make that happen, and more to the point I think he recognizes that he doesn’t have the capability to make that happen. Very few people actually like Littlefinger, there are limits to how high you can trick and stunt yourself without needing armed backup, and Littlefinger isn’t really a conquering type anyway. His position is constantly precarious and it seems dubious at best he could rally armies behind him, and equally dubious he could become king by other means.

                So I do think there are limits to his ambition.

                It doesn’t help that I still don’t know what, exactly, his goal is unless it is simply to amass power for the sake of power, or to play the game as long as hard as he can to see how high his score is when he dies. Everyone else has some kind of recognizable endgame, but I’ll be damned if I can tell what Littlefingers is.

                  For the show…I can see parallels, but the plan is definitely going to be different in at least some aspects for at least three reasons. 1) Ramsey Bolton is never going to be ruled over by LF or Sansa the way Harry might be 2) The politics of that plan are way too complex and time intense for the show to set up and 3) LF’s plan will never come to fruition in either the show or the books.             I don’t think Littlefinger is necessarily attempting to dump Bolton at the moment, or even trying to go splitsville with Cersei immediately. He’s just covering all his bases.                 April 27, 2015 at 2:46 pm

                Yeah, this action would seem to put Littlefinger openly against the Lannisters. The only explanations I can think of for this are:
                1) He thinks Cersei is so insanely furious with Tyrion that she won’t blame Sansa for Joffrey, care that Littlefinger has married a Lannister wife to a different family, or care that House Bolton’s ties to the North are strengthened at the expense of those of the Lannisters. Now, Cersei is too petty and vindictive to see the big picture, but it’s hard to see her letting all of that slide. Particularly the part about her hatred of Sansa and suspicions over her part in Joffrey’s death.

                On the face of it, this scheme gets him an ally in House Bolton, who loosely control the North, and an enemy in House Lannister, who control the crown. Stannis doesn’t really enter into it, because if he wanted Stannis to make Sansa a Warden of the North, he’d be better off keeping her safe until Stannis wipes out Bolton, then whipping her out after.

                It seems like he has risked a lot, putting his most valuable piece into play like this, and there doesn’t seem to be much that he’s gaining from doing it now.
