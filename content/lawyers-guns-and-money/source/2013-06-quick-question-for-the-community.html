      [ 115 ] June 21, 2013 | SEK

I thank any and all in advance for whatever advice you provide.

            One thing to keep in mind is that when you call a national name brand like Mayflower and ask them to move your stuff, you’re not really hiring them. You’re hiring a regional contractor that the national moving company works with in your area to do the packing and/or loading and at the same time another regional contractor that handles the unloading at your house.

            So my advice generally speaking would be to look at the contractors at both ends when thinking about the reviews, rather than reviews about the national company. It’s nice if lots of people in the Northeast had a great experience with Mayflower, but it might not mean much for your move from the West Coast to the South.

          • Green Caboose says:
            June 21, 2013 at 4:40 pm

            So this was going to be my exact question as well – and given your answer you have a challenge.

            Think about it. This is a one-time transaction. You are NOT going to be a repeat customer. If you move again you’ll pick a different company because of the regional variation in companies. Even the national companies, as others have mentioned, like Mayflower, Allied, etc are really just an almalgamation of local companies.

            And one-time transactions mean that once they load your shit on their truck you are fully committed and they can do whatever they want.

            I can see trying movingscam.come and angieslist (which I endorse) if you have no other source. But your best bet is if your employer has a company they use. Get them to set up the move for you even if you are paying for it yourself. This way you move from a “no repeat customer” situation to a “repeat customer” situation. And the treatment you get should change – very likely you’ll get an entirely different contact name and number. And any complaints route them through your new employer.

        Eric is dead on above that the big names (United, Mayflower, North American, etc.) utilize local agents that actually do the work. So your research should focus on the quality of the agent.
