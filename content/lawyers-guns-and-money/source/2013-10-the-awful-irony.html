      The awful irony

      [ 22 ] October 27, 2013 | SEK

      Lou Reed’s Magic & Loss has helped me deal with many an untimely death in my life, and now it must do the same for his.

      But there are things that we can’t know,
      Maybe there’s something over there,
      Some other world that we don’t know about,
      I know you hate that mystic shit.

      So say we all.

      12. Nora Carrington says:

        It’s funny but not surprising to me that Andrea Dworkin — also dead much too young — should be mentioned here. There was something over/past/beyond about Reed’s take on misogyny and violence. He wasn’t immune, exactly, just more knowing, more transparent to himself. “Somebody else would have broken both of her arms.” He wouldn’t, but he knew how the trigger between anger and violence felt under his finger. I think he saw the people in the broken women and the tranny whores and the rent boys most missed. I loved him, I loved his music, I appreciate who he was to the culture. Reed mattered. Not too many people get to say that and have it be true.
