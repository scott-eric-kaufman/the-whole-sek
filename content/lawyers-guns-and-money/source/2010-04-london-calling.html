      London Calling

      [ 45 ] April 6, 2010 | SEK

      This Friday, I’ll be leaving my home country of Kenya to visit England for this conference. I’ve never been to England and am a nervous traveler, so you can do the math: I’m not sure what to do for the five days I’ll be there without a conference to hold my hand. In other words, if you had four days in England to do whatever you wanted with, what would you do?

      34. Over in Brussels says:
        April 8, 2010 at 2:50 am

        First things — stop at a news agent and buy the current issue of “Time Out” and a “mini London A to Z.” “Time Out” is a weekly guide to almost everything that’s on in London. You will find lots of choices ranging from art exhibits to guided walks to raves and concerts. Surely something will catch your fancy. The A to Z is a street atlas, and the best way to navigate London’s streets without a GPS. It’s the locals’ bible, so you won’t look like a tourist holding one. Make sure you get a current Tube map, too.

        A few things to do —

        South Bank stroll from Borough Market (taste cheeses at Neals Yard, buy a sausage roll or scallops sauted with bacon or anything else that catches your eye) to Tate Modern and across Millennium Bridge to Saint Pauls. Borough Market is adjacent to Southwark Cathedral and just across from London Bridge station. It’s open on Friday and Saturday, and there are many little stands selling artisinal food products. Great atmosphere (through crowded these days) and nearly endless grazing and tasting opportunities.

        Wander through Regents Park and down Marylebone High Street (stopping for fish and chips at the Golden Hind). This is my old neighborhood, and the High Street is one of my favorite bits of non-tourist London. The park is huge and varies from formal gardens – including the incredible rose garden at the center – to a semi-wild, open space on the north side, towards the zoo and Camden Town.

        Walk up Charing Cross Road, browsing the booksellers.

        Have a drink at a posh cocktail bar (Artesia at the Langham, the Soho Hotel, the Lonsdale in Notting Hill, the bar at the National Portrait Gallery for a funky view of the London skyline)

        Visit Camden Market on the weekend — great people watching and a mecca for edgy and current fashion.

        Take in a West End show (but buy your tickets at the half price ticket stand at Leicester Square. Ignore the 1/2 price shops on the edge of the square; go to the real deal in the square itself.)

        Use Time Out to find a stand-up comedy show. Lots of opportunities most nights. My favorite was a small club in Hampstead, but if you follow the recommendations in TO you probably won’t go wrong.

        Otherwise, just walk walk walk walk walk.

        Favorite Places to Visit for History and Culture –

        British Museum

        Tower of London

        V&A (Victoria and Albert) Museum

        Westminster Abbey

        Imperial War Museum

        Pubs (well, these are historical and pub culture is a crucial bit of English life) Ye Olde Cheshire Cheese (Fleet Street), the Charles Lamb (Islington), the Lamb and Flag (near Covent Garden, off Charing Cross Road)

        Eating –

        You can find a cheap (relatively) but good take away lunch (sandwiches, soups, salads) at any Prt Manger or EAT (Try the bacon butty for breakfast!)

        Me, I like to take in the occasional Full English breakfast at any hole-in-the-wall caf. It’s likely to be as good or better than that on offer at your hotel, and it’ll be 1/2 to 1/3 the cost…

        Tea at Browns or the Dorchester — just because it’s London and you should.

        The food halls at Harrads are worth wandering through as well… I can’t say I’ve ever bought anything there, but they are amazing…

        Restaurant recommendations:

        Lahore Kebab House (dirt-cheap Indian/Bangladeshi, East End)
        Maze (Modern European, Mayfair)
        Al Waha (Lebanese, Notting Hill)
        Mohsen (Iranian, Hammersmith)
        The Fox (gastropub, The City)
        Konstam (Modern British locavore, Kings Cross)
        Bocca di Lupo (Italian, Piccadilly Circus)
        Moti Mahal (upscale Indian, Covent Garden/Holborn)
        Wild Honey (Modern European, Mayfair)

        Away from London –

        Day Trips — Hampton Court, Canterbury, Greenwich, York

        Other London restaurant recommendations:-
