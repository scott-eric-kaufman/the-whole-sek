                    I’m gonna break down the progression so you can see how I got to my question:

                    1) The free market in theory under a perfectly competitive environment a seller only makes a profit if he provides more utility to another than he put in himself. This is how the modern economy is supposed to work, we specialize, increase the value of our labor contributions, and then trade with others who have done the same. The mechanic gets really good at fixing cars so he doesn’t have to get really good at providing everything he needs. This is preferable because it places everyone in a position where they can only secure their ends by being the means to another’s.

                    2. I acknowledge that there are all sorts of factors, externalities, transaction costs, etc., that can cause the market to reach suboptimal and distorted results. This can and has resulted in inequity in the past and will result in inequity in the future.

                    3. The distortions are caused because in reality competition is not perfect and the incentives and disincentives are skewed.

                    4. Therefore, a solution would require a change of incentives. If you do not change the incentives, these distortions will consistently draw those who would use them for their personal gain at the expense of all others.

                    So the question is how would a government solution shift the incentives so that there will not still be a huge incentive to focus on cheating people rather than satisfying them?

                    You can say that there is a track record of government doing this, but I see an even stronger track record of increased government intervention leading mostly to increased effort by businesses to exploit government, not abide by it.

                    So how do you make government so that shitty people can’t make money off of their power within the government? If you do not explain how to shift the incentives, you do not explain how the government is a solution, let alone a “complete solution”.
