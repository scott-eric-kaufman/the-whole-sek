      Food recommendations on I-10 East from Corona, CA to Houston, TX

      [ 57 ] July 28, 2013 | SEK

      Since this is now my personal Xanga/livejournal/Myspace page — and because the recommendations on the podcast thread were so exquisite I’m already listening to them while packing — I thought it wouldn’t hurt to ask. I’m planning on doing 10 hours days from Corona to El Paso and then El Paso to Houston, so I don’t want to stop for a long lunch — nor will the three howling cats in my rented SUV — so places I can call in advance and eat on the way would be preferred. I’ve been looking online for such places, but they’re either far too fancy, which will kill my cats, or they’re perfect but have been recently burned down. I shit you not: four of the five places that initially excited me have burned down in the past few months.

      So … recommendations? Including on the drive itself, places to stay that are cat-friendly, etc. Basically any recommendation you have that doesn’t amount to “DON’T BE DEAF YOU ANNOYING LITTLE COCKSUCKER.” (Which, when you think about it, is probably The Donalde’s way of saying he’s going to miss me when I’m gone. Not enough to stop sexually harassing his students, but enough to feel a pang above his waistline when he does so, you know, in the part of his brain where shame ought to live.)

          • Pinko Punko says:
            July 28, 2013 at 8:02 pm

            Luling is better than the Lockhart places. They are hit and miss, when they are on they are on, but Luling is just more consistent. SEK can easily get in and get out- it is really right off I-10 (2 miles). If he wanted to blow an extra 40 minutes (20 minutes up to Lockhart and back) he could do the rounds, but only if City Market were disappointing. My experience is that Smitty’s is better than Kreuz, but this is only n=3-4 times. Blacks was very off the day I went.

            Lockhart places Smitty’s and Kreuz Market have the same style sausage as Luling, but Smitty’s is way too fatty- so much rendered fat in the sausage pours out when you break the casing and then the meat is loose. It tastes good, but is not really that appetizing. Kreuz is the opposite- it is slightly dry on the sausage front and less tasty. Smitty’s ribs can be good, but in general I find that they are underdone- they have a good rub with a little brown sugar on the ribs (lots of places in Texas are basically just salt and pepper). Luling ribs similarly have a little brown sugar but are usually cooked long enough to render more fat and have them be more tender- so these are tops for Texas-style spareribs. Black’s is very hit and miss. I have had amazing brisket at both Kreuz and Smitty’s but also average. Both are higher heat so the brisket is really quite good given that it only really goes 5-6 hours. I don’t know if Luling is also higher heat, but they are just much more consistent, and usually a better crust. Since this will be right on SEKs drive, that would be the way to go. Unless he is going through on a Sunday (they are closed). There is a place across the street from City Market in Luling that is probably OK. There is a very fine line between average and amazing Texas BBQ, and since even top places are hit and miss, the idea is to just lower the hell out of your expectations and then if you hit a good day, you are blown away.

        For cheap eats in Houston, Gorditas Aguascalientes is the place to go–in fact, it’s probably the place to go, cheap or or otherwise.         As for cat-friendly hotels, whenever we travel with our cats, we always stay at La Quintas. We’ve never had a bad experience with one. In Houston, the Robin’s Nest Bed and Breakfast is pet-friendly and quite a nice play to stay.
