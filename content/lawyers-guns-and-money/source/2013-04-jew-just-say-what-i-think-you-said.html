      Jew just say what I think you said?

      [ 88 ] April 18, 2013 | SEK

      Jew know that’s considered offensive to those people? It’d be good for them if you made a sincere apology:

      But we shouldn’t criticize him, because he “has brought and will continue to bring integrity and common sense to the House of Representatives,” and this is basically what passes for “integrity” and “common sense” in Oklahoma. No? It isn’t?

      Maybe the people of Oklahoma should ask their representatives to wipe those snide “you’re not supposed to say that aloud” looks off their faces. I’m not actually offended by this sort of casual racism — I grew up Jewish in the South after all — nor am I even surprised by it. The father of one of my best friends nicknamed me “Good One,” not because he thought I was one of the good ones, but because he thought I was the good one.

      What offends me isn’t the casual racism, then, but the smile that accompanies the apology. Because Dennis Johnson is a man who knows his audience and is absolutely certain he’s delivering a punchline.

          Actually, being good at business has only recently (literally within living memory) become a trait to be openly lauded and encouraged among our WASPy upper classes.

          Sullying your hands with mere COMMERCE was considered, right up until about WWII, to be, well… gauche. You could make a lot of money and be a person of power and influence, but you would always be a bit… nouveau riche. A bit of a johnny-come-lately. People of real breeding didn’t need to think about money (they inherited it, or at least, got it from respectable sources, like marrying into it or from rents on their land), and they certainly would never be so crass as to talk about it or (heavens!) brag about their negotiating prowess. Trying to drive a hard bargain with a social peer was quite the faux pas.

          Said upper classes were in fact, historically, rather suspicious of people in business. In 19th century England you actually had a weird dynamic where people would give impassioned speeches in the House of Lords about how Parliament existed to promote trade, by god, trade with the world, and the British would buy and sell the world, by god, because they were the best traders in the world. And then they’d turn around and confess privately to their colleagues how horrified they were that all these… tradesmen were undermining the nobility and the established social order, and good heavens, Lord Fancypants daughter was going to have to MARRY one of them and what has it come to that a gentleman like that cannot maintain himself in style by charging his peasants rent anymore, they’ve all gone to live in those AWFUL cities and turned to crime and drink, no doubt.
